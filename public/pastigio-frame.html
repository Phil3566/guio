Quick Start Guide

Gift Setup — Just Unboxed? Start Here

Step 1: Plug in the frame using the included power cord. The screen will turn on automatically.
Step 2: Select your language (English, Spanish, French, etc.).
Step 3: Connect to WiFi — tap your network name, then type the password. Must be 2.4GHz WiFi (not 5GHz).
Step 4: Enter your name and where the frame is (e.g., "Mom's Kitchen" or "Living Room").
Step 5: If it asks to update, tap Yes and wait. This may take a few minutes.
That's it! Your frame is ready. Now add a family member so they can send you photos (see below).

Connect to WiFi — The #1 Problem

IMPORTANT Your WiFi must be 2.4GHz. The frame cannot connect to 5GHz networks. This is the most common reason it won't connect.
Can't see your network? Ask someone to check if your router has a separate 2.4GHz network name. Many modern routers combine 2.4GHz and 5GHz into one name — the frame may not find it.
See it but can't connect? Double-check the password. Tap the eye icon to show what you're typing. WiFi passwords are case-sensitive.
Network requirements: Must be 2.4GHz • Must not be hidden • Must use WPA2 (not WPA3) • Channels 1–11 only
Still stuck? Ask a family member to create a separate 2.4GHz network name on the router, then connect the frame to that name.

Add Family Members

Step 1: Tap the frame's screen once to open the menu.
Step 2: Tap "Add friend" (the little person icon with a + sign).
Step 3: A code appears on screen. It's valid for 12 hours.
Step 4: Call, text, or email this code to your family member.
Step 5: They download the free "Frameo" app on their phone (iPhone or Android).
Step 6: They open the app, tap "+", and enter the code.
Done! They can now send you photos from anywhere in the world. Each family member needs to do this separately.
Code expired? No problem. Just generate a new one by tapping "Add friend" again. There's no limit.

For Family Members — How to Send Photos

Step 1: Download the free "Frameo" app on your phone (iPhone or Android).
Step 2: Get the code from the frame owner (they'll call or text it to you).
Step 3: Open the app → tap "+" → enter the code.
Step 4: Select photos → tap Send. You can send up to 10 photos at a time (or more with Frameo+).
Step 5: Photos appear on the frame within seconds!
Tip: The 10-photo limit is per batch, not total. Just send multiple batches — there's no limit on how many photos the frame can hold.

Quick Reference

Viewing Photos

Browse: Swipe left or right on the screen to see the next or previous photo.
Quick menu: Tap the screen once. A menu appears with icons for settings, friends, albums, and more.
React: Double-tap the screen to send an emoji reaction. The person who sent the photo will see it in their app.
Slideshow: Photos rotate automatically. Change the speed in Settings → Slideshow → Timer.

Managing Photos

Delete a photo: Tap screen → tap the trash icon. This permanently removes the photo.
Hide a photo: Tap screen → tap the hide icon (eye with a line). The photo stays in storage but won't show in the slideshow.
Adjust how a photo fits: Tap screen → tap Adjust → drag to reposition what part of the photo you see.
Fill vs. Fit (most common confusion): Fill = photo fills the whole screen, but the edges may get trimmed. Fit = you see the whole photo, but there will be bars on the sides. Tip: Landscape photos taken on your phone usually look best.
Sort order: Settings → Slideshow → Photo display order. Choose: Date received, Date taken, or Shuffle.

Display & Sleep

Brightness: Settings → Display → drag the brightness slider. Max brightness is best for well-lit rooms.
Sleep schedule: Settings → Display → Sleep schedule. Default: off at 11pm, on at 7am. Adjust to match your routine.
Power off: Tap screen → tap the Power icon (top right) → tap Power off.
Sleep mode: Tap screen → tap the Power icon → tap the Moon icon. The frame goes dark but still receives photos.
Turn on: Press and hold the power button on the back of the frame. It's near the center — you may need to use both hands.

Albums & Organize

Create an album: My Photos → My Albums → tap "+" to create a new album.
Add photo to album: While viewing a photo, tap screen → tap the "Add to album" icon.
Important: Deleting an album does NOT delete the photos inside it. The photos stay in your library.

Videos & Sound

Play video: Play/pause controls appear automatically when a video comes up in the slideshow.
Volume: Tap the speaker icon on screen to adjust volume.
HEADS UP Videos play with sound during the slideshow. This can be startling, especially at night. To mute: Settings → Slideshow → Video volume → Mute.
Video length: Free: up to 15 seconds per video. Frameo+ subscription: longer videos.
Widgets: Show clock, weather, or date on photos: Settings → Slideshow.

Loading Photos Without the App

SD card: Insert SD card → tap Import to copy photos to the frame. Card must be FAT32 format, max 32GB.
USB drive: Insert USB stick (FAT32 format) → tap Import.
From computer: Connect USB cable → enable in Settings. Mac NOT supported — Windows and Linux only.
Tip: SD card is the easiest way to load hundreds of photos at once — no subscription needed, no 10-photo limit.

Care & Info

Troubleshooting

Won't turn on: Check the power outlet works. Try a different cord. Unplug for 5 minutes, then replug. Press and hold the power button on the back for 10 seconds.
Frozen screen: Press and hold the power button on the back until the frame turns off. Unplug for 5 minutes, then replug.
Hard reset (last resort): Find the tiny reset hole on the back. Push a toothpick or paperclip in and hold for 5 seconds. WARNING This deletes all photos and settings.
WiFi keeps disconnecting: Restart the frame. Make sure you're on 2.4GHz WiFi. If it drops weekly, the WiFi chip may be faulty — contact the seller.
Photos not arriving: Check WiFi is connected (look for the WiFi icon in the top bar). Ask the sender to check if the frame shows as "online" in their Frameo app.
Screen flickers: Lower brightness in Settings → Display. If flickering persists, contact the seller for replacement.
Touch not responding: Peel off any screen protector. If the top of the screen is dead, try rotating the frame upside-down to reach menus. Contact the seller.
Clock/time wrong: The frame has no internal battery. After a power outage, the clock resets. Reconnect to WiFi to auto-set the time, or set it manually in Settings.
About the "don't unplug" warning: This sounds scary but just means: use the power button to shut down, don't yank the cord. If you lose power in a storm, your photos are safe. You may just need to reset the clock.
Frame completely dead after months? This is likely a hardware failure. Contact the seller through your Amazon order for warranty replacement.

WiFi Problems

Can't find network: Must be 2.4GHz (not 5GHz), must not be hidden, must use WPA2 (not WPA3).
Wrong password: Tap the eye icon to show the password as you type. WiFi passwords are case-sensitive.
Router combines 2.4 & 5GHz: Ask someone to create a separate 2.4GHz network name in router settings. Connect the frame to that name.
Connected but no internet: Restart both the router and the frame.
Drops WiFi every few days: This is a known issue. Restarting the frame usually fixes it temporarily. If it happens constantly, the WiFi chip may need warranty replacement.

Backup & Storage

Backup to SD card: Settings → Backup → Backup to external storage. Card must be FAT32 format, max 32GB.
Auto backup: With an SD card inserted, the frame can back up automatically within 30 hours of any changes.
Cloud backup: Requires Frameo+ subscription ($1.99/month).
Import from SD card: Insert card → tap Import. Great for loading lots of photos at once.
What is FAT32? It's a format for memory cards. Most new SD cards up to 32GB are already FAT32. Cards larger than 32GB may need to be reformatted — ask a family member for help if needed.
